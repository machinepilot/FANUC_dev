GENERAL CHARACTERISTICS OF THE MACHINE

The press brake VERDE model, which is a product range with models from 1250 to 2050 mm and from 40 to 60 tons, is the result of constant technological research and evolution in the field of modern metal bending. The system for controlling the movements of the ram, done with a combined electronic-mechanical function, is the most advanced technology found in press brakes today.

These types of machines are used for deforming by bending flat sheet metal into a variety of shapes and manners and are composed of and feature the following parts:

movable top beam, to which the punch is attached, driven by 2 high-load recirculating ball screws controlled by electric motors;

fixed lower beam (not movable) with attached die;

structural body;

machine backgauge completely driven by CNC;

machine is controlled and programmed by CNC with quick and simple controls;

high level of safety in the work area owing to compliance with the latest safety regulations and usage of the finest devices to ensure it.

Most important components

MACHINE BODY

The structure that connects all the fundamental parts of the press brake is composed of several steel parts welded together and its stability is verified according to FEM calculations. It consists of two steel sides fastened together on the top and bottom to further improve stability. The most important feature the structure must possess is high stability, as various equipment rests on it such as the backgauge, the top beam, the platform, etc.

LOWER BEAM

The lower beam in housed in a special recess, firmly fastened to the machine body and screwed on it. This type of connection makes the beam an integral part of the machine body and its function is:

to give greater stability to the machine body;

to facilitate changing the type of bending tool;

to facilitate proper positioning of the backgauge;

to change the position of the front support shelves according to the tools chosen by the user;

TOP BEAM (RAM)

The top beam is connected to the machine body by two high-load recirculating ball screws with joints that can rotate. This part of the machine moves during the bending phase and is therefore firmly connected to the motors (system patented Nr. MI2012A000052).

The platform (beam table) and ram are perfectly parallel with two linear incremental transducers controlled by the programmer which, through electronics, control moment by moment the position of the ball screws and subsequent balancing, acting singularly on the motors that command Y1 and Y2.

This system makes parallel bending possible even when bending is not centred in relation to the machine axis.

N.B.: it is important to emphasise that the joints connected to the beam found on the nuts of the ball screws allow the beam to be inclined to a determined degree. Inclining is done by controlling the motors separately from the CNC; this is how conical bending is done.

In linear difference, the degree of inclination cannot be greater than the maximum value of 10 mm, so it does not interfere with the structural parts of the machine.

BACKGAUGE

The backgauge of the press brake allows pieces to be shaped to specific designs because it supports and positions the sheet metal according to the desired heights. In fact, its position determines the bending edge, supports the piece when the moving part during the bending phase of the sheet metal is in the work area, etc…

It plays a fundamental role during the bending phase and its precision, the regularity of its positioning and structural stability are decisive.

The distance of the backgauge from the machine axis (adjusting X-axis), its height in reference to the positioning of the sheet metal (adjusting R-axis) and the displacement along the inner frame of the machine body of the turrets where the fingers are lodged, are controlled by the machine programmer.

The backgauge features these functions:

highly precise positioning of the axes while moving owing to the recirculating ball screws and gears located along the machine axes;

quickly moving axes owing to the use of very reactive motors that allow for quick upper positioning, in particular when the press brake is inserted in a robotised cell;

positioning regularity.

A backgauge with these characteristics offers countless practical advantages:

owing to the continuous programming of axes X and R, and their reliability and regular positions, large lots of complex pieces can be produced without significant wasting of time due to position tolerances when the backgauge stops in the upper part;

the possibility of automating the press brake’s work cycle, which basically means the press brake can be inserted in a workcell due to the high and flexible positioning speeds. Production time can therefore be reduced in an automated system or for processing large pieces which require continuous adjustment of the axes on broad displacement strokes;

continuous programming of all machine axes reduces setup times for X and R axes to a minimum;

the possibility of setting up different workstations on the press brake owing to the motorised movement of axes Z1 and Z2;

its structure and positioning on the machine make the backgauge stable and able to give structural stability to the entire movement system.

MACHINE CONTROL PANEL

The press brake is equipped with a control panel where all the controls for the machine’s functions are found. The Touch Screen control system allows users to touch and activate various machine controls displayed on a virtual panel; only a few buttons are found there physically. The control panel is equipped with dedicated ports where an industrial keyboard and mouse can be connected and interfaced, to improve interaction with the control.

When there is not enough time to refer to the software guide, the online guide can be retrieved with the (F1) key in order to view the explanation of the active control or be able to search for a particular function.

DESCRIPTION OF THE MACHINE

During the bending phase, the structural body of the machine is under stress for the strain caused by the tools and movement of the high-load ball screws located on it. At all times, all its functions are monitored so that the press brake always functions correctly and optimally.

The most important functions and characteristics which are guaranteed during the bending phase follow below.

Automatic recovery of pressure on the side frames

Positioning optical scales on a structural part not subject to strain and well anchored on a stable part of the press brake, we can obtain a reading that is free of the small pressures that the machine structure undergoes. In this way, the optical scales transmit the exact position of the Y1 and Y2 axes to the motors.

Recovery of pressure on the top (and lower) beams

During the bending phase, the structural parts are subject to small variations in form which must be cancelled in order to bend properly. Thanks to the combined use of several sensors able to quantify the entity of variations in the beam’s form.

Automatic compensation for variations in the thickness to be bent

Using the information on pressure that the motors exercise on the high-load ball screws, the exact thickness of the sheet metal to be bent can be measured so that the CNC software is able to regulate itself to calculate all the variables necessary for bending.

Automatic measurement of the length of the sheet metal to be bent

Using the abovementioned information the sensors are able to communicate, the machine can measure the exact length of sheet metal that it is bending. In this manner, the CNC is able to fine-tune the calculation of the variables necessary for bending.

Checking the power required for bending

.

After setting the values of the sheet metal thickness, its length, the bending angle of the piece, the material’s resistance and type of tool used on the machine’s CNC function, the programmer is able to calculate the power necessary to bend it.

The exact measurement of the length of the sheet metal and its thickness can be calculated by the sensors on the machine, making the calculation precise.

Adjusting the bending angle (OPTIONAL only upon request)

The bending angle can be verified and corrected thanks to the laser device integrated into the system for measuring the angle. When bending is done, the top beam, now under slightly less bending pressure, allows the bended piece to “float” on the die in order to be measured by the laser device’s optical reader. If the measured angle is different from what is necessary, another bending can be done, calculated by the CNC, to correct the previous bending height.

Speed adjustment

The press brake’s CNC function allows all the speeds involved in the bending phase to be checked to make the machine more functional and safe while in use.

By changing some of the CNC parameters, all the position variables and the following speeds can be programmed:

APPROACH SPEED

This is the speed with which the top beam moves until it reaches the speed change point.

POINT OF SPEED CHANGE OR SAFETY

This is the point in which the speed of the top beam changes from approaching to working speed which ranges between 0-10 mm/sec. This point is calculated and programmed according to the thickness of the sheet metal to ensure work area safety.

SHEET METAL PINCHING POINT

When requested, this point is used where the top beam rests on the sheet metal that has not yet been bent, and consequently on the die, to allow the backgauge to move backwards. Once the backgauge has moved back, bending continues until completed.

WORKING SPEED

This is the speed that the top beam has while it bends the sheet metal. It is established by the machinery directive to ensure work area safety; as previously mentioned, it can be programmed between 0-10 mm/sec.

RETURN SPEED

This is the speed with which the top beam returns to the upper dead point (UDP) or to a programmed point that carries out the same function, after sheet metal bending is completed.

Stopping precision of the top beam descent

All top beam movements are controlled by the machine’s CNC function and its positioning precision is +/-

0.01 mm.

Adjusting the upper dead point (UDP) of the top beam

The upper dead point is where the ram, having finished the bending cycle, returns upward and then stops. The distance from its bending point can be programmed to reduce wasted time significantly in a bending sequence. Therefore, according to bending needs, the UDP point can be programmed in a lower point to save time.

Possibility of interrupting an automatic bending cycle

By using a switch, an automatic bending cycle can be interrupted to bend a single piece manually.

Inclining the top beam

By programming from the CNC, the top beam can be inclined so that conical bending can be done. The default inclination can be 10 mm, meaning the beam can be inclined making the two axes Y1 and y2 independent and staggering them with this linear measurement.

LIFTING, TRANSPORT AND INSTALLATION CONDITIONS

LIFTING THE PRESS

The press brake must be lifted in order to install it in the place where it will be used. This operation should not be underestimated as a wrong move and improper fastening of the press brake to the lifting apparatus can cause considerable harm to people as well as damage to the machine.

General warnings

Below follows a list of general warnings on lifting so it can be done safely. All press brake lifting and handling must be done by trained personnel.

IT IS OBLIGATORY that a person in charge of making reports be present at all times;

it is FORBIDDEN to stand in the area near the press brake during lifting, loading and unloading operations;

it is FORBIDDEN to stand in the outreach area of the crane;

it is FORBIDDEN to move the press brake using levers as it could turn over, given the location of its centre of gravity;

it is OBLIGATORY to be very careful of how the press brake is secured to the crane to prevent sudden movements due to the location of its centre of gravity; in fact, this latter is located quite forward compared to the centre line of the side view of the machine;

the space around the press brake must OBLIGATORILY be left free during moving operations to prevent risks of injury due to sudden displacements or imbalances;

it is OBLIGATORY to secure the press brake, make it inaccessible and to FORBID that anyone use it should the machine be left in the warehouse without being anchored to the ground for an unspecified amount of time.

The press brake must remain still once it is placed on the ground because its centre of gravity is located between the two supporting feet. Displacements or imbalances which may cause it to tip over can occur if it collides with moving vehicles, such as forklift trucks, cranes, etc.

it is OBLIGATORY to verify, by contacting STR s.r.l., the weight of each part that is to be assembled onto the press brake;

it is OBLIGATORY to ensure that suitable equipment for unloading and handling the machine pieces is available;

it is OBLIGATORY to check the carrying capacity of the lifting devices (bridge cranes, cranes, etc.,), as well as shackles, cables, cords and chains used for slinging and hitching onto the various parts to be assembled;

it is IMPORTANT that the device that is to lift the machine be positioned in the centre of each part to be assembled to ensure balanced lifting;

BACKGAUGE (MODEL 9S WITH 2 X-R AXES)

The range of RGS backgauges was created both following a new design concept which is called the “Plug&amp;Play” assembly system guaranteeing maximum quality and reliability, as well as the “CANOPEN” product design which allows the positioning of sheet metal to be held in its stopping points which are positioned according to the programme with predefined heights in total precision and speed. Thus, the depth of bending to be done is always perfect.

The backgauge with X and R axes is composed of a single-unit (X axis) arranged parallel to the bending line and where stops holding the sheet metal are inserted along its length. Another vertical (R axis) movement follows the X axis which allows the stops to follow the variations in height along the sheet metal edge or even that of the lower tool.

The backgauge ensures great sturdiness and rigidity. It is equipped with state of the art motors which ensure not only high precision and speed but above all, exceptional silence. It also features an anti-collision system which unlocks the rear stop, to prevent damage to the relevant upright, in the event of accidental impact or manoeuvring errors.

The properties and some of the advantages of the backgauge were listed previously. However, it must be clarified that the functionality of a backgauge also depends on its type. There are in fact various models of backgauges which can be used according to the characteristics of the piece to be bent.

The beam descends to its theoretical depth and stays in the safety position to prevent excessive bending. The software makes the top beam light which allows the sheet metal to spring back. The springback of the sheet metal pushes the top beam upward until it finishes the movement so as to prevent the sheet metal from slipping away during this phase. The software then calculates the necessary depth to correct the initial bending and then completes bending.

CONSOLE PEDALS

Ascent pedal

Pressing the ascent pedal lifts the upper beam. This command has priority over that of descent, in fact, simultaneously pressing the two pedals causes the machine to open. In the event that an emergency stop has occurred or a guard has been opened, it is possible to execute the up command again only after having carried out the reset procedure. The reset procedure, described in detail later, consists in restoring the initial working safety conditions, such as closing the rear guard, then pressing the rear gate reset button and then the general reset button, located on the console panel (pedal board).

Descent pedal

Pressing the descent pedal controls the descent of the upper beam. It is called 3 way because it has 3 positions. The first position is that of rest (stationary), the second commands the descent of the ram and the third is reached by pressing the pedal further down. When the third position is reached, the upper beam is stopped during the descent. Restarting the upper beam is only possible after returning the pedal to position no. 1 so that the initial conditions are restored and the pedal can be pressed again.

It is important to underline that position no. 2 is separated from no. 3 thanks to a pressure device. To exceed position no. 2, a specific force is required which must not exceed 350N.

GENERAL BENDING TECHNIQUES

The bending techniques used with the bending press are basically of two types:

1 - BENDING IN AIR OR FREE

2 - COINED OR PUNCHED BEND

2 - COINED OR PUNCHED BEND

Below we will see the fundamental difference between the two bending types. It is good to specify that every time a sheet is bent, it comes up against a natural phenomenon called elastic return. The sheet, due to internal tensions and its own molecular disorder, retains a part of its elasticity when the force that deformed it ceases.

Not all sheets have an identical behaviour, for example stainless steel sheets have a more solid elastic return compared to iron sheets with R = 40 ÷ 45 Kg / mm, the difference is attenuated by increasing the thickness of the sheet to be bent.

According to the phenomenon just explained, elastic return, and the thickness of the sheet to be bent, we distinguish the two bending techniques listed above.

AIR BENDING

In this type of bending, the punch deforms the sheet and impresses it with an angle according to the requirements of the particular piece to be executed. During this bending technique the sheet retains a part of its elasticity, different for each material, and depending on the thickness and type of material, it is necessary to make a more closed angle than the one you want to obtain. Part precision thus depends on residual material elasticity and is determined by the punch penetration depth.

There are additional advantages using this bending technique:

it requires a limited bending force and consequently a low tonnage machine can be used;

allows the execution of different bend angles with the same equipment;

allows you to bend thick sheet metal better.

It is important to consider that the use of slots having openings (width of "M" in the following table) smaller than a value between 6 ÷ 14 times the sheet thickness, can cause superficial defects such as cracks near the external radius of the sheet. On the other hand, by using larger M openings, coarse and much larger bending radii are obtained.

N.B. It is specified that the optimum working cavity is 8 to 10 times the sheet thickness.

N.B. It is specified that the optimum working cavity is 8 to 10 times the sheet thickness.

COINED BENDING

Unlike the previous system, this bending has the purpose of cancelling out the elasticity of the material, forcing it to acquire the shape of the punch tip by means of a real moulding action.

The advantages of this bending technique are:

a high angular precision of the bends performed;

elimination of the phenomenon of elastic return;

possibility to obtain angles with sharp corners unthinkable with the previous technique.

However, there are disadvantages generated by coin bending which make it less and less used.

presses with a high tonnage are required, since the bending force is 5 to 8 times greater than bending in air;

the required equipment has high costs given its precision, which determines the successful outcome of the bent piece, and the lack of flexibility, given that each profile corresponds to a different piece of equipment;

these folds have a good result for sheets with a maximum thickness of 1.5 mm.
